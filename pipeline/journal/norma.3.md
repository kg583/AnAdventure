God, this place is a mess. I really need to keep better care of my workstations here, but I think the Nether conspires against me. There was nylium *everywhere* when I arrived, and part of the floor had burned up who knows how long ago. Not much I can do about that latter part, I suppose, what with all the fire and lack of water.

Speaking of lack of water, my weeks away *did* give enough time for my last experiment to prove fruitful. See, I've been thinking about... death, lately. Our special relationship with it. We die, but we come back, hardly the worse for wear. For all other beings, death is the end, and I have a theory as to why we are the exception. The erratic valleys of dust and bones which can be found here... we call the earth there "soul sand", and for good reason!


(Well, *I* called it "soul sand", and everyone else agreed because it was better than Draco's "brown stuff".)


See, wisps of souls are trapped inside the silt; if you tread on them, you can hear their... cries. It's unsettling and I try not to think about it too much. Anyway, the sheer volume of sand tells me that not just Netherkind souls are bound here, but those from the Overworld and perhaps other realms as well. With so many souls in one place, it's no surprise that *something* strange would happen, and lo, this is the origin of the ghasts!

I captured a ghast during my last excursion into the soul sand valley and only got exploded a little bit.
